Step 1

Check the Scene and the Person

Make sure the scene is safe for you and the person in need. Look for any potential dangers such as traffic, fire, or hazardous materials.

Check the person for responsiveness. Tap their shoulder and shout, "Are you okay?" If they do not respond, call 911 or ask someone else to call.

If the person is not breathing or is only gasping, begin CPR immediately. Place the person on their back on a firm, flat surface.

Step 2

How to Perform CPR

1. Place the heel of one hand on the center of the person's chest. Place the heel of your other hand on top of the first hand. Keep your elbows straight and position your shoulders directly above your hands.

2. Use your upper body weight (not just your arms) as you push straight down on (compress) the chest at least 2 inches. Push hard and fast at a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute.

3. After every 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths. Tilt the person's head back slightly to open the airway. Pinch the nose shut and place your mouth over the person's mouth to make a complete seal. Blow into the person's mouth to make the chest rise. Deliver the rescue breaths, one after the other.

Step 3

How to Use an AED

1. Turn on the AED and follow the visual and/or audio prompts.

2. Open the person's shirt and wipe their bare chest dry. Attach the AED pads to the person's bare chest. Place one pad on the upper right side of the chest and the other pad on the lower left side of the chest, a few inches below the left armpit.

3. Make sure no one is touching the person. Tell everyone to "stand clear." Push the "analyze" button on the AED. The AED will check the person's heart rhythm.

4. If the AED recommends a shock, make sure no one is touching the person and tell everyone to "stand clear." Push the "shock" button. After the shock is delivered, immediately resume CPR, starting with chest compressions.
